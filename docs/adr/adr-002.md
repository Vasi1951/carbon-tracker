# ADR-002: Carbon Calculation Methodology (GHG Protocol aligned)

## Status

Approved

## Context

We need to define a consistent, accurate, and standards-compliant methodology for calculating carbon emissions from user activities. Aligning with international standard frameworks like the **Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Protocol** is critical for auditability, comparability, and trust.

## Decision

We align our domain calculations directly with the GHG Protocol corporate standard, using emission factors to translate raw activity telemetry into CO2e (Greenhouse Gas CO2-equivalents) emissions.

### 1. Emission Formula

All calculations follow the standard activity-based calculation methodology:

$$\text{Emissions (kgCO2e)} = \text{Activity Data (Amount)} \times \text{Emission Factor (co2ePerUnit)}$$

- **Transport**: Distance (km) \* factor (kgCO2e/km)
- **Food**: Mass (kg) \* factor (kgCO2e/kg)
- **Energy**: Energy Consumed (kWh) \* factor (kgCO2e/kWh)
- **Consumption**: Units/Price \* factor (kgCO2e/unit)

### 2. Category Structuring & Scopes

To enable GHG Protocol reporting, activities map to the following categories:

- **TRANSPORT**: Maps to Scope 1 (Direct - company vehicles) or Scope 3 (Indirect - employee commuting, business travel).
- **ENERGY**: Maps to Scope 2 (Indirect - purchased electricity, heating, cooling).
- **FOOD / CONSUMPTION**: Maps to Scope 3 (Indirect - value chain: purchased goods, food consumption).

### 3. Data Integrity & Rounding Invariants

- **Decimal Precision**: All final emissions are rounded to exactly **two decimal places** in the domain layer to prevent precision drift and present clear user-facing reports.
- **Factor Year Limits**: Factors must specify a year between **2000 and 2030** to ensure historical context and prevent stale/obsolete factors from being used.

## Consequences

- **Pros**: Direct alignment with GHG reporting protocols. Auditable structure.
- **Cons**: Requires keeping factor databases up to date with region-specific datasets (EPA, DEFRA, etc.).
